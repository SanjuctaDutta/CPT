Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a Mars rover?

    A Mars rover is a robotic vehicle designed to explore the surface of Mars. These rovers are equipped with various scientific instruments to conduct experiments and gather data about the Martian environment and geology.

  • What are the main objectives of sending rovers to Mars?

    The main objectives of Mars rovers are to search for signs of past or present life, study the planet's geology and climate, understand the potential for human colonization, and advance our knowledge of the Red Planet.

  • Why do scientists study NEOs?

    Scientists study NEOs to monitor their orbits and assess the risk of potential impacts with Earth. Understanding NEOs is crucial for planetary defense and for preparing strategies to mitigate any potential threats.

  • What is Astronomy of the Day (APoD)?

    Astronomy of the Day (APoD) is a website and service operated by NASA that presents a new and interesting astronomical image or photograph each day, along with a brief explanation written by professional astronomers.

  • What kind of content can you expect to find on Astronomy of the Day?

    On Astronomy of the Day, you can find a wide variety of content, including images of celestial objects (e.g., stars, galaxies, planets, nebulae), descriptions of astronomical phenomena, information about ongoing space missions, and insights into the universe's wonders.
